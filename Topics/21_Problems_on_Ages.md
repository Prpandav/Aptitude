# Problems on Ages - Fundamentals

## 1. Formulas

**General Age Relation:**
If the current age is $x$:
- Age $n$ years ago = $x - n$
- Age $n$ years hence (after $n$ years) = $x + n$

**Ratio and Ages:**
If the current ages of A and B are in the ratio $a:b$, then:
- Age of A = $ax$
- Age of B = $bx$

**Average Age:**
$$ \text{Average Age} = \frac{\text{Sum of Ages of all persons}}{\text{Number of persons}} $$

---

## 2. Key Concepts

- **Age Difference is Constant:** The difference between the ages of two persons remains the same at any point in time. If A is 5 years older than B today, A will still be 5 years older than B 10 years later.
- **Ratio Shifts:** While the difference remains constant, the ratio between ages changes over time.
- **Algebraic Representation:** Most problems are solved by translating the given statements into linear equations.

---

## 3. Shortcuts

- **The Difference Equalization Method:**
  If the ratio of ages of A and B at one time is $a:b$ and at another time is $c:d$, and the time gap is $T$ years:
  1. Calculate the difference $(b-a)$ and $(d-c)$.
  2. If they are not equal, multiply the ratios to make the differences equal.
  3. The "units" of change in the ratio will then correspond directly to the years $T$.

- **Option Substitution:** In many multiple-choice exams, plugging the given options into the conditions of the problem is faster than solving equations.

---

## 4. Common Mistakes

- **Wrong Time Frame:** Using the "future" ratio with "present" ages or vice versa. Always identify the reference point (usually "now").
- **Adding/Subtracting only one side:** When moving forward or backward in time, remember to add or subtract the years from *everyone's* age, not just one person.
- **Confusion in "Times":** Mistaking "A is 3 times older than B" for $A = 3B$ when it sometimes implies $A = B + 3B = 4B$ (though in most aptitude tests, "3 times as old" and "3 times older" are treated as $A = 3B$).

---

## 5. Solved Examples

### The Ratio Shift
The ratio of ages of A and B is 4:5. After 6 years, the ratio becomes 6:7. Find their present ages.
*Solution:* 
Difference in units: $6-4 = 2$ and $7-5 = 2$.
So, 2 units = 6 years $\implies$ 1 unit = 3 years.
Present ages: $4 \times 3 = 12$ and $5 \times 3 = 15$.

### The Past-Future Relation
A father said to his son, "I was as old as you are at the present at the time of your birth". If the father's age is 38 years now, what was the son's age 5 years back?
*Solution:* Let son's current age be $x$. Father's age at son's birth = $38 - x$.
Given: $38 - x = x \implies 2x = 38 \implies x = 19$.
Son's age 5 years ago = $19 - 5 = 14$ years.

## Easy Level

### Problem
The sum of the ages of a mother and her daughter is **50 years**. Also, 5 years ago, the mother's age was **7 times** the age of the daughter. What are their present ages?

### Solution

### Step 1: Define variables
Let daughter's current age = $x$
Mother's current age = $50 - x$

### Step 2: Set up the equation for 5 years ago
(Mother - 5) = 7 \times (Daughter - 5)
$(50 - x - 5) = 7(x - 5)$

### Step 3: Solve for $x$
$45 - x = 7x - 35$
$80 = 8x$
$x = 10$

### Final Answer
Daughter is **10 years** old, Mother is **40 years** old.

---

## Medium Level

### Problem
The ratio of the ages of A and B is **3:4**. Ten years ago, the ratio of their ages was **4:7**. Find their present ages.

### Solution

### Step 1: Set up ratios
Present: $3x$ and $4x$
10 years ago: $\frac{3x - 10}{4x - 10} = \frac{4}{7}$

### Step 2: Cross-multiply
$7(3x - 10) = 4(4x - 10)$
$21x - 70 = 16x - 40$
$5x = 30$
$x = 6$

### Step 3: Calculate ages
A = $3 \times 6 = 18$
B = $4 \times 6 = 24$

### Final Answer
**A is 18, B is 24**

## Placement-Level Problem

### Problem
A person's age is **125%** of what it was 10 years ago, but **83.33%** of what it will be after 10 years. What is his present age?

### Solution

### Step 1: Use the first condition
Let present age = $x$
$x = 125\% \text{ of } (x - 10)$
$x = 1.25(x - 10)$
$x = 1.25x - 12.5$
$0.25x = 12.5$
$x = 50$

### Step 2: Verify with the second condition (Optional)
83.33% is $\frac{5}{6}$
Is $50 = \frac{5}{6} \times (50 + 10)$?
$50 = \frac{5}{6} \times 60 = 50$. (Correct)

### Final Answer
**50 years**

## 6. Practice Set

### Beginner Questions (10)
1. If A is 20 years old and B is 30 years old, what was the ratio of their ages 5 years ago?
2. The sum of ages of 5 children born at the intervals of 3 years each is 50 years. What is the age of the youngest child?
3. A father is 3 times as old as his son. After 15 years, he will be twice as old as his son. Find the father's present age.
4. Rahul is 4 years older than his sister Maya. If the sum of their ages is 32, how old is Maya?
5. The ratio of present ages of P and Q is 5:8. If the difference between their ages is 12 years, find Q's age.
6. A man is 24 years older than his son. In two years, his age will be twice the age of his son. The present age of his son is?
7. Six years ago, the ratio of the ages of Kunal and Sagar was 6:5. Four years hence, the ratio of their ages will be 11:10. What is Sagar's age at present?
8. The total age of A and B is 12 years more than the total age of B and C. C is how many years younger than A?
9. At present, the ratio of the ages of Maya and Chhaya is 6:5. After 15 years, the ratio will be 9:8. Maya's present age is?
10. The product of the ages of Ankit and Nikita is 240. If twice the age of Nikita is more than Ankit's age by 4 years, what is Nikita's age?

### Intermediate Questions (5)
1. The sum of the ages of a father and his son is 45 years. Five years ago, the product of their ages was 34. Find their present ages.
2. A is two years older than B who is twice as old as C. If the total of the ages of A, B and C be 27, then how old is B?
3. Present ages of X and Y are in the ratio 5:6 respectively. Seven years hence this ratio will become 6:7 respectively. What is X's present age?
4. The ratio of the father's age to his son's age is 7:3. The product of their ages is 756. The ratio of their ages after 6 years will be?
5. A's age is 1/6th of B's age. B's age will be twice of C's age after 10 years. If C's eighth birthday was celebrated two years ago, then what is A's present age?

### Challenge Questions (2)
1. The ratio of the ages of a man and his wife is 4:3. After 4 years, this ratio will be 9:7. If at the time of marriage, the ratio was 5:3, how many years ago were they married?
2. In a family, a couple has a son and a daughter. The age of the father is three times that of his daughter and the age of the son is half of his mother. The wife is 9 years younger than her husband and the brother is 7 years older than his sister. What is the age of the mother?

## 7. Answers & Hints

**Beginner:**
1. **3:5** (15 and 25)
2. **4 years** ($x + x+3 + x+6 + x+9 + x+12 = 50$)
3. **45 years** ($3x+15 = 2(x+15) \implies x=15$)
4. **14 years** ($x + x+4 = 32$)
5. **32 years** ($3x = 12 \implies x=4$)
6. **22 years** ($(x+24)+2 = 2(x+2)$)
7. **16 years** ($\frac{6x+10}{5x+10} = \frac{11}{10} \implies x=2$. Present = $5x+6$)
8. **12 years** ($A+B = B+C+12 \implies A-C=12$)
9. **30 years** (Difference 1 unit = 3 years. $6 \times 5 = 30$)
10. **12 years** ($x(2x-4) = 240$ or test factors of 240)

**Intermediate:**
1. **39 and 6** (Factors of 34 are 34 and 1. Ages 5 yrs ago: 34, 1. Present: 39, 6)
2. **10 years** ($2x+2 + 2x + x = 27 \implies 5x=25$)
3. **35 years** (1 unit = 7 years. $5 \times 7 = 35$)
4. **2:1** ($21x^2 = 756 \implies x^2=36 \implies x=6$. Ages: 42, 18. After 6 yrs: 48, 24)
5. **5 years** (C now = 10. B after 10 yrs = 40. B now = 30. A = 30/6)

**Challenge:**
1. **12 years ago** (Present ages: 32, 24. Marriage: $\frac{32-n}{24-n} = \frac{5}{3}$)
2. **60 years** (Let D = $x$. F = $3x$. M = $3x-9$. S = $x+7$. Son is half of mother: $x+7 = \frac{3x-9}{2} \implies 2x+14 = 3x-9 \implies x=23$. Mother = $3(23)-9 = 60$)

## 2. Phase 1: Arithmetic Engines

### Percentages

* **Successive Percentage Change:** $\text{Net \%} = a + b + \frac{a \times b}{100}$
* **$A$ is what \% of $B$:** $\frac{A}{B} \times 100$
* **Percentage Growth/Decline:** $\frac{\text{Final} - \text{Initial}}{\text{Initial}} \times 100$

### Ratio & Proportion

* **Compound Ratio:** If $a:b$ and $c:d$, the compound ratio is $ac:bd$.
* **Combining Ratios:** If $A:B = x:y$ and $B:C = y:z$, then $A:B:C = x:y:z$.

### Averages

* **Core Formula:** $\text{Average} = \frac{\text{Sum of Observations}}{\text{Number of Observations}}$
* **Weighted Average:** $\frac{n_1w_1 + n_2w_2}{n_1 + n_2}$

### Profit, Loss & Discount

* **Profit/Loss \%:** Always calculated on Cost Price (CP). $\frac{\text{SP} - \text{CP}}{\text{CP}} \times 100$
* **Discount \%:** Always calculated on Marked Price (MP). $\frac{\text{MP} - \text{SP}}{\text{MP}} \times 100$
* **Dishonest Dealer Rule:** $\text{Profit \%} = \frac{\text{True Weight} - \text{False Weight}}{\text{False Weight}} \times 100$

### Simple & Compound Interest

* **Simple Interest (SI):** $\text{SI} = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$
* **Compound Interest Amount:** $A = P(1 + \frac{R}{100})^n$
* **2-Year SI/CI Difference:** $\Delta = P(\frac{R}{100})^2$
* **3-Year SI/CI Difference:** $\Delta = P(\frac{R}{100})^2 \times (\frac{R}{100} + 3)$

### Problems on Ages

* Let present age $= x$.
* Age $n$ years ago $= x - n$.
* Age $n$ years from now $= x + n$.

## 3. Phase 2: Core Application

### Time & Work / Pipes & Cisterns

* **Total Work:** $\text{Efficiency} \times \text{Time}$
* **Two Workers Together:** $T = \frac{xy}{x + y}$
* **One Fills, One Empties (where $y > x$):** $T = \frac{xy}{y - x}$
* **MDH Rule (Chain Rule):** $\frac{M_1 \times D_1 \times H_1}{W_1} = \frac{M_2 \times D_2 \times H_2}{W_2}$

### Time, Speed & Distance

* **Unit Conversion:** $\text{km/hr} \rightarrow \text{m/s}$ (Multiply by $\frac{5}{18}$) | $\text{m/s} \rightarrow \text{km/hr}$ (Multiply by $\frac{18}{5}$)
* **Average Speed (Equal Distances):** $\text{Avg} = \frac{2xy}{x + y}$
* **Relative Speed:** Same Direction $= |S_1 - S_2|$. Opposite Direction $= S_1 + S_2$.

### Boats & Streams

* **Downstream ($D$):** $B + S$
* **Upstream ($U$):** $B - S$
* **Boat Speed in Still Water ($B$):** $\frac{D + U}{2}$
* **Stream Speed ($S$):** $\frac{D - U}{2}$

### Mixtures & Alligations

* **Alligation Cross Rule:** $\frac{\text{Quantity of Cheaper}}{\text{Quantity of Dearer}} = \frac{D - M}{M - C}$
* **Fluid Replacement Formula:** $\text{Final Pure Liquid} = \text{Initial Volume} \times (1 - \frac{\text{Replaced Volume}}{\text{Total Volume}})^n$

### Probability

* **Core Rule:** $P(E) = \frac{\text{Favorable Outcomes}}{\text{Total Sample Space}}$
* **Complement Rule:** $P(\text{At least 1}) = 1 - P(\text{None})$
* **Independent Events:** $P(A \text{ and } B) = P(A) \times P(B)$

### Permutations & Combinations

* **Permutation (Arrangement):** $^nP_r = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$
* **Combination (Selection):** $^nC_r = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$
* **Circular Permutations:** $(n-1)!$
* **Handshake Formula:** $\frac{n(n-1)}{2}$

## 4. Phase 3: Math & Spatial Systems

### HCF, LCM & Number Systems

* **Product Rule:** $\text{HCF} \times \text{LCM} = A \times B$
* **Fraction LCM:** $\frac{\text{LCM of Numerators}}{\text{HCF of Denominators}}$
* **Fraction HCF:** $\frac{\text{HCF of Numerators}}{\text{LCM of Denominators}}$
* **Sum of first $n$ natural numbers:** $\frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

### Geometry & Mensuration

* **Circle:** $\text{Area} = \pi r^2$ | $\text{Circumference} = 2\pi r$
* **Triangle:** $\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$
* **Cylinder:** $\text{Volume} = \pi r^2h$ | $\text{Curved Surface Area} = 2\pi rh$
* **Cone:** $\text{Volume} = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2h$ | $\text{Slant Height } l = \sqrt{r^2 + h^2}$
* **Sphere:** $\text{Volume} = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$ | $\text{Surface Area} = 4\pi r^2$

### Clocks & Calendars

* **Angle between hands:** $\theta = |30H - 5.5M|$
* **Leap Year Rule:** Must be divisible by 4. If a century year (ends in 00), must be divisible by 400.
* **Odd Days:** Ordinary year = 1 odd day. Leap year = 2 odd days.

## 5. The Deadliest Runtime Traps

1. **The Cost Price Inversion:** Profit/Loss percentages are ALWAYS calculated on the Cost Price (CP), never on the Selling Price (SP).
2. **The "At Least" Overcount:** In Probability/P&C, never calculate overlapping "at least 1" groups directly. Calculate: $1 - P(\text{None})$.
3. **The Base Year Swap:** In Data Interpretation % Growth questions, the denominator is always the INITIAL (oldest) year.
4. **Left/Right Spatial Slip:** In circular seating arrangements, if they face OUTWARD, *their* left is *your* right.
5. **The Gender Assumption:** In Blood Relations, never assume someone is male or female just because their name is a specific gender. Wait for the pronoun/relation constraint (e.g., "brother", "wife").

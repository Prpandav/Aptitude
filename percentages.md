## Formula

**Basic Percentage:**

$$
\text{Percentage} = \left( \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Total}} \right) \times 100
$$

**Percentage Change:**

$$
\text{Percentage Change} = \left( \frac{\text{Final Value} - \text{Initial Value}}{\text{Initial Value}} \right) \times 100
$$

**Product Stability (Price vs Consumption):** If price increases by $P$%, consumption must decrease by:

$$
\text{Decrease Percentage} = \left( \frac{P}{100 + P} \right) \times 100
$$

## Key Concepts

- **Base Identification:** The denominator is always the baseline or original value being compared against (the value following the word "than").
- **Fractional Scaling:** Percentages are fractions with a fixed denominator of 100.
- **Successive Changes:** Sequential percentage adjustments cannot be directly added or subtracted. They follow a compounding effect: Net Change = $A + B + \frac{AB}{100}$.

## Shortcuts

- **The Anchor Method:**
  - Move decimal 1 spot left for $10\%$
  - Move decimal 2 spots left for $1\%$
- **Essential Fraction Equivalents:**
  - $12.5\% = \frac{1}{8}$
  - $16.66\% = \frac{1}{6}$
  - $33.33\% = \frac{1}{3}$
  - $9.09\% = \frac{1}{11}$

## Mistakes

- Assuming a successive increase and decrease of the same percentage cancels out (it results in a net decrease).
- Picking the wrong baseline value for standard comparison questions.

## Important Questions

1. **The Compounding Problem:** If a value drops $20\%$, then drops another $10\%$, what is the total change?
   - *Solution:* Let base = 100. After -20% $\rightarrow$ 80. After -10% of 80 (which is 8) $\rightarrow$ 72. Total drop = $28\%$.
2. **The Constant Commodity Cost:** Price of sugar up by $25\%$. Keep expense same.
   - *Solution:* $\frac{25}{100+25} \times 100 = \frac{25}{125} \times 100 = 20\%$ reduction in consumption.

# 2. Solved Examples

## Easy Level

### Problem

In a test, a student scores **54 marks out of 75**. What is their percentage?

### Solution

### Step 1: Identify the values

* Part = 54
* Total = 75

### Step 2: Apply the formula

$$ \left(\frac{54}{75}\right) \times 100 $$

### Step 3: Simplify the fraction

Divide numerator and denominator by 3:

$$\frac{54}{75}=\frac{18}{25}$$

### Step 4: Calculate

$$\frac{18}{25} \times 100 = 72\%$$

### Final Answer

**72%**

---

## Medium Level

### Problem

If A's salary is **25% more** than B's salary, by what percentage is B's salary less than A's salary?

### Solution

### Step 1: Assume a base value

Let B's salary = 100

### Step 2: Calculate A's salary

$$100 + 25\% \text{ of } 100 = 125$$

### Step 3: Find the difference

125 - 100 = 25

### Step 4: Percentage difference relative to A

$$\left(\frac{25}{125}\right) \times 100 = 20\%$$

### Final Answer

**20%**

---

## Placement-Level Problem

### Problem

Due to a **20% reduction** in the price of sugar, a consumer can buy **5 kg more sugar** for ₹600. Find the original price per kg.

### Solution

### Step 1: Understand the scenario

* Total money remains constant = ₹600
* Price decreases by 20%

### Step 2: Find money saved

$$20\% \text{ of } 600 = 120$$

The saved ₹120 allows the buyer to purchase an extra 5 kg.

### Step 3: Reduced price per kg

$$\frac{120}{5} = 24$$

Reduced price = ₹24/kg

### Step 4: Find original price

Since ₹24 is 80% of the original price:

$$\text{Original Price} = \frac{24 \times 100}{80} = 30$$

### Final Answer

**₹30 per kg**
